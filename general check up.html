Routine Dental Checkup

What is a Routine Dental Checkup?

A routine dental checkup is a preventive examination where the dentist checks your teeth, gums, tongue, and mouth to detect problems early before they become painful or expensive to treat. It is the most important step in maintaining lifelong oral health.

Why Dental Checkups are Important

  • Detect cavities before they cause pain
  • Identify gum disease early
  • Check for oral infections or swelling
  • Detect bad breath causes
  • Screen for oral cancer signs
  • Monitor previous fillings, braces, or dentures
Most serious dental problems start silently. A simple checkup today can save you from tooth loss, infection, or expensive treatment tomorrow.

What happens during your visit

  • Full oral examination of teeth and gums
  • Checking for cavities or decay
  • Assessment of gum health
  • Checking bite alignment and jaw movement
  • Examination of tongue and soft tissues
  • Professional advice on brushing and diet

Who needs regular checkups?

  • Children and adults
  • Patients with fillings or dentures
  • People with bleeding gums
  • People with diabetes or medical conditions
  • Anyone wanting to keep their smile healthy

How often should you visit?

  • Every 6 months for most patients
  • Every 3–4 months if you have gum disease
  • Before major dental treatments
  • If you feel pain, swelling, or sensitivity
Regular checkups reduce dental costs, prevent tooth loss, and help maintain a clean confident smile.
